EARLY LIFE & FAMILY

Imran Khan was born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore into an affluent Niazi Pashtun family. His father, Ikramullah Khan Niazi, was a highly qualified civil engineer, while his mother, Shaukat Khanum, belonged to a deeply respected Sufi family in Mianwali. His maternal family possessed a rich history of producing exceptional cricketers, which unknowingly laid the foundation of Imran's future sports journey.

Growing up, Imran was a quiet and relatively shy boy, surrounded by four loving sisters. He received his elite education at the prestigious Cathedral School and Aitchison College in Lahore, where he excelled in both sports and academics. His talent was undeniable, leading him to Worcester, England, for higher school education.

In 1972, Imran Khan was admitted to Keble College, Oxford, where he studied Philosophy, Politics, and Economics (PPE). Graduating in 1975, his academic years in the UK broadened his vision and global outlook, shaping the powerful personality that would later lead a nation of 240 million people.

THE CRICKET LEGEND

Imran Khan made his first-class cricket debut at the age of 16 in Lahore and shortly represented the national team in 1971. Initially regarded as a medium-fast bowler, he worked tirelessly on his physical strength and bowling action. By the late 1970s, he emerged as one of the fastest, most feared bowlers in international cricket, spearheading Pakistan’s bowling attack.

In 1982, Imran was appointed the Captain of the national team. His uncompromising leadership, unmatched tactical awareness, and ability to discover young raw talent (like Wasim Akram and Waqar Younis) transformed Pakistan from a fragmented team into a world-class force. He commanded respect and instilled absolute self-belief in his squad.

The crown jewel of his cricketing career was the 1992 Cricket World Cup. At the age of 39, coming out of retirement on the popular demand of his country, he led his "Cornered Tigers" from the brink of elimination to an emotional and historic World Cup victory at the Melbourne Cricket Ground, securing Pakistan's first-ever world title.
